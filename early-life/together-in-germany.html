Together in Germany

How They Met

The exact story of how Emilie and Adam first met is uncertain. Roger believes their paths crossed around 1926, and the impression is that they did not know each other well before becoming involved. Their ages—15 for Emilie and 21 for Adam—help explain that distance. Religious background also posed a challenge: Emilie came from a Protestant family, while Adam was Catholic.

Their relationship led to a pregnancy. During this time, Emilie was sent to Goldbach to live with an Utz family. As the birth approached, she went to a birthing home in Heidelberg, where Egon was born on August 31, 1927.

“What I remember is that Mom’s mother sent her away; Mom’s father did not know of any pregnancy—had he known, he would have been very, very upset. I believe he didn’t see Mom again until I was born. As I grew up, he completely melted. I even remember sleeping in his bed. He always kept chocolate in his night table for me and for my Großmutter. Inwardly he was a very soft person, but outwardly he put on a tough-guy act.” — Egon Schreiner

At some point, Adam and Emilie decided to marry. They first sought a ceremony in the Catholic Church, but the priest refused to marry them because Emilie was Protestant.

“They could have been married in the Catholic Church, but not at the main altar. Pop would not accept being married in a side room. He had served as an altar boy at that church.” — Egon Schreiner

Eventually, arrangements were made for a wedding in the Protestant Church in Oppau. (As an aside, after World War II, Emilie collected donations in Ridgewood to help fund a replacement stained glass window for that church, which had been destroyed during the war.) Emilie and Adam were married on May 18, 1929, at the Protestant Church in Oppau. Independent in spirit, Emilie chose a white wedding gown—a departure from the local tradition of black wedding dresses.

It is not clear how fully each family was represented at the service, but they did have both a church wedding and a civil ceremony. A post-wedding celebration photograph (location unknown) shows strong representation from both families.

After the wedding, the couple lived upstairs on Friedrichstr. in Emilie’s parents’ house. Erika remembered that the home was arranged as a two-family residence, with Emilie and Adam having their own kitchen. She spent much time there.
